Energy Price Fluctuations, Metal and Food Trends

Global Impact and
Sustainable Strategies

A project by Althaf, Brian, Evelyn, and Imelda

The dynamics of energy, metal, and food commodity prices over the past two decades reflect global market volatility, geopolitical influences, and strategic needs to maintain industrial and food security worldwide.

Global Energy Price Fluctuations: Crisis, Spikes, and Recovery

Energy prices have experienced sharp fluctuations between 2005-2024. Oil prices surged to nearly 100 USD/barrel in 2011-2012, dropped drastically to around 30-40 USD in 2014-2016, then climbed back to approximately 50-90 USD in 2022.

iNATGAS recorded extreme spikes over 280 USD/MMBtu in 2021-2022, while coal prices from Australia and South Africa peaked above 240 USD/ton. These fluctuations have major impacts on industrial costs and global energy strategies.

Fig 1: Oil, Gas, and Coal price.

The sharp energy price fluctuations reflect global economic dependency on energy sources vulnerable to geopolitical and market changes. These shifts require adaptive energy policies to ensure industrial stability and overall economic resilience. This situation also directly affects manufacturing and agribusiness sectors, which rely heavily on quality metal raw materials and mineral fertilizers.

Metal and Mineral Fertilizer Price Trends:
Reflecting Industrial and Agricultural Needs

Key metals have experienced significant price fluctuations and increases, especially since 2020. These price movements highlight major pressures on manufacturing sectors that rely on quality metal raw materials. Meanwhile, mineral fertilizers nearly doubled in price around 2008 and surged again in 2021-2022, reflecting increased demand in agribusiness. These fluctuations emphasize the importance of risk management to sustain business continuity and food production.

Fig 2: Metals and Mineral Fertilizers Trends.

The price movements of metals and mineral fertilizers showing significant pressure on manufacturing and agriculture sectors reflect the essential material needs to maintain global supply chains. These price fluctuations directly impact food production and industrial raw materials, making further changes in the food and natural resource sectors unavoidable.

Food and Natural Resource Price Fluctuations:
Key to Global Resilience

Prices of seafood vary with a stable upward trend. Beef prices rose from 2.5 USD/kg to over 5 USD/kg by 2024. Major agricultural products like peanuts peaked at 2,400 USD/ton and continue to rise. Plantations such as Arabica coffee surged to 11 USD/kg. Vegetable oils and tobacco also experienced significant increases. Prices of processed products like fish meal remain stable. These fluctuations reveal challenges for food security and environmental sustainability requiring adaptive strategies.

Fig 3: Food and Natural Resource Price Fluctuations.

Conclusion

The price movements of energy, metals, and food commodities over the past two decades demonstrate interconnected dynamics influenced by geopolitical, economic, and environmental factors. Sharp fluctuations in the energy sector place direct pressure on manufacturing and agribusiness, which in turn affect food security and the sustainability of natural resources. Understanding these patterns and inter-sector linkages is key to formulating adaptive and sustainable strategies to face evolving global challenges.
